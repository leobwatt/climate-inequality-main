The Rich vs. The Rest of Us

Climate inequality by income means that low-income communities suffer more from the effects of climate change due to
their lack of resources and infrastructure, while high-income people produce the most CO2 yet feel little of the effects.

Almost 40% of all CO2 emissions come from the top 10% wealthiest people on Earth. This inequality in carbon emissions is especially unfair, considering they are the least affected by climate change.

This discrepancy in emissions is better highlighted in this graph showing the same data but per capita, so the total emissions divided by the amount of people in each catagory.
